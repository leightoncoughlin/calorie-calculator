Extra Information


In order to calculate your TDEE or total daily energy expenditure we must know your BMR or basic metabloic rate. The following formula was used to calculatate the BMR

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation:
For men:
BMR = 10W + 6.25H - 5A + 5
for women:
BMR = 10W + 6.25H - 5A - 161

Where:
W = Weight
H = Height
A = Age

The value obtained from this equations is the estimated number of calories a person can consume in a day to maintain their body-weight, assuming they remain at rest. This value is multiplied by an activity factor (generally 1.2-1.95), dependent on a person's typical levels of exercise, in order to obtain a more realistic value for maintaining body-weight. Now that your TDEE has been calculated we can then add or subtract 250 or 500 calories depending your goals to give you an estimate to work with.

Please be aware this is just an estimate and should only be used as a baseline that you will later adapt based on your results. It is reccomened to consume the calories mentioned above depending on your goal and weigh yourself every morning and adapt your plan if neccessary. for example, if after a month you are still gaining weight when you had planned on maintaining, reduce your calories by 250 and see how this affects your weight moving forward.
